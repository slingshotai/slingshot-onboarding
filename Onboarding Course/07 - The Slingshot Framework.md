# The Slingshot Framework

## Why This Matters Now

You've just installed and run your first skill. Brand Voice sits in the **Story** area of ecommerce — how your brand communicates. But Story is one of seven areas that determine whether an ecommerce business grows or stalls.

The Slingshot Framework is the map. Every skill in the SlingshotAI catalogue builds into one or more of these seven areas. Understanding the framework helps you see where your business is strong, where it's weak, and which skills to prioritise next.

## The Seven Areas

Every ecommerce business needs all seven working together:

### 1. Sell
Constant curation of high-demand products that generate profit. Uses the Product Quadrant (Knowledge vs Demand) to evaluate what you're selling. The goal: move every product toward high demand + high knowledge.

### 2. Story
Your brand messaging only matters where your story and your customer's story overlap. The Brand Voice skill you just ran lives here. Key diagnostic: the We vs You ratio on any page — how much are you talking about yourself vs talking about them?

**Go deeper:** [[05C - The Story Overlap]] — the full methodology behind finding and using your brand's overlap, with worked examples, exercises, and an audit checklist.

### 3. Tech Stack
Your website, email platform, CMS, and all associated technology must work together to provide a familiar buying experience. Mobile-first. Simple. Integrated.

### 4. Marketing
Eight pillars of digital marketing: Email, Mobile, Paid Media, Search, Content, Referral & Partnerships, Social, Feedback & Strategy. Uses the **FUEL framework** to build maturity across all eight:

- **Foundation** — The boring stuff that must work: email sequences, baseline metrics, conversion essentials
- **Unlock** — Diversify. Test new channels so you're not vulnerable to one platform
- **Elevate** — Optimise what's working. Advanced segmentation, CRO, LTV modelling
- **Leapfrog** — Experiment boldly. AI innovations, emerging platforms, interactive experiences

Most businesses are strong in 2-3 marketing areas and neglect the rest. The power is in the 4x8 matrix: every marketing area at every FUEL stage.

### 5. Optimise
Increasing the chances of a visitor buying. Three pillars: Easy to find (navigation), Find the answer (product pages as mini-websites), Clear CTA. Everything tested and backed by data.

### 6. Experience
The entire post-purchase journey. From the thank-you page to transactional emails to the unboxing moment. The goal: turn a routine delivery into a "wow" that drives repeat business and referrals.

### 7. Growth
Building flywheel-type growth by maximising three variables: Number of Customers, Average Order Value, and Customer Order Frequency. Strategies include upselling, bundling, loyalty programmes, and referral systems.

## Two Guiding Principles

Everything in Slingshot is filtered through two questions:

1. **Does it move the needle?** Prioritise tasks with the biggest impact on growth.
2. **Can I consistently hit an 8 out of 10 executing this?** Choose actions that are sustainable and high quality, not everything at once.
