# Slingshot Framework — FAQ

## What is the Slingshot Framework?

A seven-part methodology for ecommerce growth: Sell, Story, Tech Stack, Marketing, Optimise, Experience, Growth. Built from decades of experience building and selling ecommerce businesses. It's the system that underpins every SlingshotAI skill.

## Where should I start?

Two guiding questions help you prioritise:
1. "Does it move the needle?" — what will have the biggest impact right now?
2. "Can I consistently hit an 8/10 executing this?" — is it sustainable?

For most ecommerce businesses, the biggest quick wins are in **Optimise** (is your site converting?) and **Story** (does your messaging resonate?). That's why Moby (mobile audit) and Brand Voice are the first skills we built.

## What's the difference between Slingshot and FUEL?

Slingshot is the overall framework (7 areas covering the whole business). FUEL is the marketing-specific methodology within the Marketing area of Slingshot. FUEL has four progressive stages (Foundation → Unlock → Elevate → Leapfrog) applied across eight marketing channels.

## What is the Product Quadrant?

A tool from the Sell area. It maps products on two axes: Knowledge (how well you and the customer understand it) vs Demand (how much the market wants it). The goal is to move every product to Q1 (high knowledge + high demand) through education and better presentation.

## What is the Story Overlap?

The core principle from the Story area. Your brand messaging only matters where your story and your customer's story intersect. The quick diagnostic: count "we/our/us" vs "you/your" on any page. If it favours "we," you're talking to yourself, not the customer.

## What does FUEL stand for?

Foundation → Unlock → Elevate → Leapfrog. Four stages of marketing maturity that must be worked through in order. Foundation is the boring stuff that must work. Unlock is diversifying channels. Elevate is optimising what works. Leapfrog is bold experimentation.

## Why does the order matter in FUEL?

Skip Foundation and your Leapfrog experiments have no stable base. Skip Unlock and you're efficient in one channel right before it becomes unprofitable. The stages build on each other — stability, then options, then efficiency, then advantage.

## What are the 8 marketing areas in FUEL?

Email, Mobile, Paid Media, Search, Content, Referral & Partnerships, Social, Feedback & Strategy. Most businesses are strong in 2-3 and neglect the rest, which creates vulnerability.

## How do I know which FUEL stage I'm at?

Map your business onto the 4x8 matrix. For each of the 8 marketing areas, honestly assess: do you have Foundation in place? Have you Unlocked alternatives? Are you Elevating what works? The matrix shows exactly where your gaps are.

## What are the three growth variables?

From the Growth area: Number of Customers (acquisition), Average Order Value (upselling, bundling), and Customer Order Frequency (repeatability, loyalty). Flywheel growth comes from all three compounding together.

## I'm overwhelmed — where do I literally start?

Run Moby on your main product page. It takes 5 minutes and gives you a scored checklist of what's working and what's broken. Fix the Tier 1 issues (foundation stuff). Then ask SAM to teach you about your Moby score — "sam, explain my Moby results." That'll lead you naturally to the next thing.
