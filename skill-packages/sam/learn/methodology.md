# Slingshot Framework — Methodology

## The Product Quadrant (Sell)

A 2x2 grid for evaluating products:

- **X-axis:** Demand (how much the market wants it)
- **Y-axis:** Knowledge (how well the company AND customer understand it)

| Quadrant | Knowledge | Demand | What it means |
|---|---|---|---|
| Q1 | High | High | Best products. People want them and understand them. |
| Q2 | Low | High | High demand but knowledge gap. Educate to move to Q1. |
| Q3 | Low | Low | Hard to convert. Low trust, low understanding. |
| Q4 | High | Low | Well understood but nobody wants it. |

**Strategy:** Move everything toward Q1.
- Increase Knowledge: build authority (training, content, blogs, videos), educate customers
- Increase Demand: improve presentation, packaging, perceived value. Measure search intent (lead measure) not just sales (lag measure)

## The Story Overlap (Story)

A Venn diagram with two circles:
- **Circle 1:** Your brand story (who you are, why you exist, what you believe)
- **Circle 2:** Your customer's story (their needs, desires, problems, aspirations)
- **The Overlap:** Where both circles meet. This is where ALL messaging should operate.

**Key insight:** Customers care profoundly about their story but very little about ours. Our founding year, our awards, our team photos — that's all our circle. The customer doesn't see it.

### The We vs You Ratio

Quick diagnostic for any page:
1. Count every "we," "our," "us"
2. Count every "you," "your"
3. If the ratio favours "we," the page is too brand-centric

### Finding the Overlap — Three Steps

1. **Define your brand story** — Write your WHY in one paragraph. Not what you sell — why.
2. **Map the customer's story** — Review mining (pull 20 reviews, extract WHY people buy), Image Buckets (visual representation of customer's world), The Customer's Movie (describe the moment before they visit your site)
3. **Find the overlap** — Draw the Venn. Write the overlap in a single sentence. This becomes the foundation of every message.

### Applying It Everywhere

The overlap isn't just for copywriting:
- **Product descriptions:** Feature → Benefit → Outcome (the benefit bridges brand story to customer story)
- **Headlines:** Verb + Object + Sexiness (Netflix formula — no "we" in sight)
- **Homepage:** Gateway to solving the customer's problem, not a brochure about you
- **About page:** Should lead back to the customer's story
- **Packaging:** Physical experience should match the emotional overlap
- **Social media:** Post from the overlap, not the brand circle

## The FUEL Matrix (Marketing)

FUEL is a 4x8 matrix — four stages of maturity across eight marketing areas.

### The Four Stages (Sequential — Must Be Done In Order)

1. **Foundation** — Stability. Basic systems that work even when ads don't. Email sequences, baseline metrics, conversion essentials.
2. **Unlock** — Options. Diversify channels so you're not dependent on one. Test alternatives.
3. **Elevate** — Efficiency. Optimise what's working. Advanced segmentation, automation, LTV modelling.
4. **Leapfrog** — Advantage. Bold experiments. AI, emerging platforms, interactive experiences.

### The Eight Marketing Areas

1. Email (sequences > blasts, "sell the click")
2. Mobile (SMS, push, app)
3. Paid Media (diversify, don't over-rely)
4. Search (benefits + problems, not just product names)
5. Content (one video → audio → blog → social snippets)
6. Referral & Partnerships (ask customers, build systems)
7. Social (community, not broadcast)
8. Feedback & Strategy (know your numbers: CAC, LTV, AOV)

### Why Sequential Matters

- Skip Foundation → your Leapfrog experiments have no stable base
- Skip Unlock → great efficiency in one channel, then it becomes unprofitable and you have nothing else
- Foundation gives stability. Unlock gives options. Elevate gives efficiency. Leapfrog gives competitive advantage.

### Three Mistakes FUEL Prevents

1. **Advanced tactics on shaky foundations** — can't run AI personalisation without a working welcome sequence
2. **Over-investing in one area** — Elevate-level Paid Media but Foundation-level Email means one algorithm change kills you
3. **Chasing innovations without infrastructure** — Leapfrog is sexy but only works on top of solid F-U-E

## The Three Optimisation Pillars (Optimise)

1. **Easy to find** — Frictionless navigation. Signpost customers quickly.
2. **Find the answer** — Product pages as "mini websites." Long scroll, answer every question.
3. **Clear CTA** — Visible, value-focused. "Start my assessment" not "Submit."

All changes tested and backed by data.

## The Three Growth Variables (Growth)

1. **Number of customers** — Acquisition
2. **Average Order Value** — Upselling, cross-selling, bundling
3. **Customer Order Frequency** — Repeatability, loyalty, subscriptions

Flywheel growth comes from all three compounding together.
